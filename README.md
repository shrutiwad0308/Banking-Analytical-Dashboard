1. Project Description / Purpose
The Banking Analytics Dashboard provides a comprehensive view of client portfolios, loan performance, deposit behavior, and revenue metrics across retail and business banking segments.
Built using Power BI, this dashboard enables bank management, credit teams, and relationship managers to monitor key performance indicators, analyze demographic trends, identify high-value segments, assess risk exposure, and drive data-driven decisions for optimizing lending, deposit mobilization, and client engagement.

2. Tech Stack
Power BI – Data visualization & interactive dashboard creation
Power Query – Data cleaning, transformation, and modeling
DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – KPIs, calculated measures, and advanced analytics
Microsoft Excel / CSV – Raw data handling and initial preparation
Synthetic Banking Dataset – Client-level data with anonymized real-world patterns

3. Data Source
Custom Synthetic Banking Dataset (2025) covering approximately 3,000 clients, including:
Client demographics (Joining Year, Gender, Nationality, Occupation, Income Band)
Banking relationship type (Commercial, Institutional, Private Bank, Retail)
Loan metrics (Total Loans, Bank Loans, Business Lending, Credit Card Balances)
Deposit metrics (Total Deposits, Bank Deposits, Savings & Checking Accounts)
Revenue & fees (Total Fees, Foreign Currency, Engagement Accounts)

4. Features & Highlights
➤ Business Problem
Banks often face challenges due to fragmented visibility of client portfolios, especially during growth phases. Common issues include:
Inefficient segmentation of high-value vs low-performing clients
Difficulty tracking loan and deposit balance distribution across relationship types
Limited visibility into demographic drivers of lending and deposit behavior
Reactive rather than proactive risk and revenue management

➤ Goal of the Dashboard
Provide a holistic view of loan and deposit portfolios
Analyze performance by client demographics, banking relationship, and advisor
Identify high-revenue segments and potential risk concentration areas
Support strategic decisions on cross-selling, client retention, and portfolio optimization

5. Business Impact
Enables targeted client acquisition and cross-selling strategies
Highlights high-performing segments for optimized resource allocation
Supports risk assessment by identifying concentration risks
Quantifies revenue contribution from loans vs deposits
Improves advisor performance tracking and portfolio management

6. Key Insights
Around 53% of bank loans and deposits come from Medium Income Band clients, indicating strong middle-class penetration
Retail and Private Banking dominate the portfolio with notable business lending exposure
Certain occupations and nationalities show higher loan uptake, enabling focused marketing strategies
Savings and checking accounts contribute significantly to overall deposits, highlighting the importance of retail banking
Consistent performance across joining cohorts (2013–2021) indicates stable long-term client acquisition
